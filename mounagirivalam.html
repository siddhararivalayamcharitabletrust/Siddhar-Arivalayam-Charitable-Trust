மௌன கிரிவலம் – Mouna Girivalam




Mouna Girivalam is a sacred ritual performed at the Arunachala Hill in Tiruvannamalai, Tamil Nadu, India.

Here's more information:

What is Mouna Girivalam?

Mounam means silent. Giri means Hill. Valam means coming around the hill in clock wise direction.

Mouna Girivalam, also known as Girivalam or Pradakshina, is a silent circumambulation of the Arunachala Hill. Devotees walk around the hill in silence, meditating and focusing on their spiritual journey.

Significance
The ritual is considered sacred because the Arunachala Hill is believed to be a manifestation of Lord Shiva. The hill is said to represent the fire element, and the circumambulation is believed to purify the mind, body, and soul.

When to Perform
Mouna Girivalam can be performed at any time, but it's most auspicious during the full moon day (Pournami) and the new moon day (Amavasya). Many devotees also perform the ritual on special occasions like the Karthigai Deepam festival.

How to Perform
1. Begin at the Arunachaleswara Temple and proceed clockwise around the hill.
2. Maintain silence throughout the ritual.
3. Focus on your breath, meditation, or prayers.
4. Remove your shoes before starting the ritual.
5. Offer prayers and worship at the various lingams and shrines along the way.

Distance and Duration
The circumambulation path is approximately 14 kilometers long. The duration of the ritual varies depending on the individual's pace and the number of stops they make along the way. On average, it takes around 3-4 hours to complete the ritual.

Benefits
Performing Mouna Girivalam is believed to bring numerous spiritual and physical benefits, including:

- Purification of the mind, body, and soul
- Spiritual growth and self-realization
- Relief from mental and physical ailments
- Increased focus, concentration, and mental clarity
- Inner peace and calmness

Overall, Mouna Girivalam is a powerful and transformative spiritual practice that offers a unique opportunity for self-reflection, growth, and connection with the divine.
